1. Purpose

This SOP defines how work moves from idea to production across Product, Design, and Development. It creates clarity and alignment across all roles, ensures designs are based on clear requirements rather than assumptions, and reduces rework, miscommunication, and scope drift. By having consistent quality checks at each stage and defining responsibilities, expectations, and handover points, it enables efficient, predictable delivery of high-quality features.

It ensures work is well-scoped before design begins, validated before development, and tested before release — protecting time, improving outcomes, and making it easier for everyone to do their best work.

2. Roles & Responsibilities

Each role owns specific stages. No stage should progress without its owner completing their responsibilities.

RolePrimary Responsibilities
StakeholderOriginates ideas; shares insights with PM and Technical Lead; reviews and approves designs; provides directional feedback.
PMWrites clear tickets and PRDs; manages stakeholder communication; coordinates the design–dev handover; drives shipping.
Technical LeadProvides technical insights during stakeholder conversations; validates feasibility; supports PM on prioritisation; attends handover meetings if necessary.
DesignerPicks up tickets; seeks clarification when needed; designs all states and edge cases; presents options; actions feedback; conducts design review post-build.
DeveloperFlags constraints early; builds to spec; raises blockers promptly; records final Loom on completion.
QATests all functionality and edge cases; logs bugs with clear reproduction steps; signs off before design review.

3. The Process

Fourteen steps across five phases. Work moves forward only when the current step is complete.

Phase ABrief & Scoping
Step 1Idea received from stakeholder
Owner: Stakeholder PM Technical Lead
  • Stakeholder shares the idea or request with the PM and Technical Lead.
  • The Technical Lead may be involved at this stage to provide early technical context or help gather insights.
  • PM captures the idea and determines whether it needs more context before a ticket can be written.
Step 2Feasibility check (if required)
Owner: PM Technical Lead Developer
  • If there are significant technical constraints that could directly affect what can be designed, PM and Technical Lead discuss with the dev team before design begins.
  • This step is only needed when constraints might meaningfully shape the design — it is not a full scoping session.
  • Findings are noted on the ticket.
Step 3Ticket / PRD written
Owner: PM
  • PM writes the ticket before design begins. A well-written ticket is what sets a feature up for success.
  • Include: goal, context, non-negotiables, what to include and what to exclude (if known).
  • For a redesign: describe specifically how it should differ from the current design.
  • 'Simplify this' is not an actionable brief. If simplification is the goal, specify which information can be removed. The designer cannot make that call without research or explicit direction.
  • If scope is intentionally open and the designer is expected to define the solution, state this clearly.
  • Ticket created in Jira and assigned to the Designer.
✅ Definition of Done: Ticket exists in Jira with sufficient detail for design to begin. Goal, context, non-negotiables, and any inclusions/exclusions are documented.
Phase BDesign
Step 4Designer picks up the ticket
Owner: Designer
  • Designer reads the ticket in full and moves it to 'In Progress' in Jira.
  • If anything is unclear at any point during the design process, the designer asks the PM directly. Questions may come up throughout — this is expected and encouraged.
Step 5Designs completed in Figma
Owner: Designer
  • Designer creates designs covering all required states: default, loading, empty, error, and edge cases.
  • Where there are meaningful design directions to consider, the designer prepares two to three options.
  • Once ready, the designer posts in the team's design chat, tagging the PM and Technical Lead, with a brief explanation of the options presented.
Step 6PM / Technical Lead review
Owner: PM Technical Lead
  • PM and Technical Lead review the designs against the brief and stakeholder expectations.
  • Feedback is provided via Figma comments or in the team chat where the work was posted.
  • Designer actions feedback and resubmits if changes are needed.
  • Once both PM and Technical Lead are satisfied, work moves to stakeholder review.
✅ Definition of Done: PM and Technical Lead have reviewed and approved the design direction.
Phase CStakeholder Review
Step 7Designs sent to stakeholders
Owner: PM Designer
  • Designs are shared as screenshots with the Figma link clearly included.
  • The message should explain the design, outline the options, list any questions for direction, and note any assumptions made.
  • Frame the share to invite specific, actionable feedback.
Step 8Stakeholder feedback & iteration
Owner: Stakeholder Designer
  • Stakeholders review and provide feedback. Designer actions it and resubmits. This loop continues until stakeholders confirm they are happy.
  • If stakeholder feedback introduces significant new scope or a fundamentally different direction: stop. The PM should reassess and update the ticket before the designer continues. Designing off an evolving brief makes it difficult to land in the right place and risks work going in circles.
✅ Definition of Done: Stakeholders have confirmed the design. No outstanding feedback or unresolved scope.
Step 9Designer records Loom walkthrough
Owner: Designer
  • Designer records a Loom walking through the final approved design.
  • Posted in the design showcase Teams chat and the product team chat with the relevant devs, so the development team can see what is coming.
Step 10Design–Dev handover meeting
Owner: PM
  • PM books the handover meeting as they have the best view of who needs to be involved in development and who should attend.
  • If the designer is to book the handover, the PM should clarify who needs to be included.
  • In the meeting: designer walks through the design, devs raise any technical constraints not yet considered, and everyone leaves with a shared understanding of what is being built.
  • Any decisions or constraints are noted on the Jira ticket before development begins.
Phase DDevelopment & QA
Step 11Development
Owner: Developer
  • Developer builds to the approved Figma design.
  • If a design question arises, raise it with the Designer directly, via a comment on the Figma file, or on the Jira ticket — whichever is most practical.
  • Raise blockers promptly rather than working around them silently.
  • Developers: this section is intentionally brief — add your own internal steps and workflow here.
Step 12QA review
Owner: QA Developer
  • Developer passes the build to QA once it is ready.
  • QA tests all functional requirements, edge cases (empty states, error states, mobile and tablet), and checks for regressions.
  • Bugs are logged on the Jira ticket with: steps to reproduce, expected vs. actual behaviour, and a screenshot or recording.
  • Developer fixes bugs and returns to QA. This loop continues until QA signs off.
✅ Definition of Done: All test cases passed. Zero open bugs. Ticket moved to 'Design Review'.
Step 13Design review of build
Owner: Designer Developer
  • Designer reviews the built feature against the approved Figma designs.
  • Discrepancies are noted on the Figma file and communicated to the Developer.
  • Developer fixes the issues. Designer does a final check to confirm.
  • Once the designer is satisfied, the Developer records a Loom walkthrough of the finished product for sharing with stakeholders — this replaces the sprint review.
✅ Definition of Done: Designer has confirmed the build matches approved designs. Dev Loom is recorded and shared.
Phase EShip & Ongoing
Step 14Ship
Owner: PM Developer
  • PM confirms readiness to ship, including any release timing considerations (e.g. feature flags, release windows).
  • Developer deploys to production and confirms on the Jira ticket.
  • PM closes the ticket and notifies relevant stakeholders that the feature is live.
✅ Definition of Done: Feature is live in production. Stakeholders notified. Ticket closed.

🌱 Ongoing: Research & Iteration

Shipping is not the finish line. We should continue collecting feedback on live features and remain open to iterating and improving. This is not a formal step but a standing commitment — if meaningful feedback surfaces, it feeds back into a new ticket at Step 1.
